Bad vs. Good Design

Task description: Pick out two pieces of design. One that you think is really good, and one that you think is really bad. Motivate your choices with a short comment.

My First Layout

Task description: Choose a design-related subject. It can be pretty much anything. A specific designer, a specific field of design or just a piece of art you enjoy. Historical or contemporary. Write a text of your own about your selected topic. The text should be written as if you were an author writing for a coffee table book on art/design. This is not an academic paper. Design a minimum of two spreads, maximum of four (4-8 pages) about your topic intended for a coffee table book about design. For this assignment it is permitted to borrow pictures from the internet.

Stylization

Task description: Take a picture, an object or a concept and simplify it. Your final design can be a pictogram, an ideogram, an icon or a minimalistic poster. Your final design should be done as vector graphics using Illustrator. It is ok to make a “popular culture stylization” taking inspiration from movies, tv-series, video games and music. Clarify and simplify. Be creative. Have fun!

Branding

Task description: Come up with a brand name and create a logotype for the following company: In the very north of Sweden, rich in forests and unspoiled nature, two childhood friends has for many years produced and sold a locally produced Gin at selected Systembolaget stores in northern Sweden. The Gin is flavoured with the juniper berries from the surrounding forests. They have never had any proper branding for the bottles though, and the labels has varied somewhat throughout the years. Now they have secured a deal for a larger launch throughout Sweden and northern Europe and need your help coming up with a brand name, logo and a visual identity. The brand is not exclusive but wants to be perceived as clean and modern but still close-to-nature.

Style Guide

Task description: Create a visual identity/style guide for your selected company. The style guide (handed in as a PDF) should be done as 12 pages with the following content:

  • Cover page
  • Spread 1 (page 2-3): Table of Contents
  • Spread 2 (page 4-5): Presentation of the company
  • Spread 3 (page 6-7): Presentation of the company logo + logo usage guidelines.
  • Spread 4 (page 8-9): Presentation of the company typography, brand colours and other graphic elements (such as patterns and textures).
  • Spread 5 (page 10-11): A mood board displaying the intended style of photos and other visuals for the brand.
  • Back cover: use for whatever you find most suitable
